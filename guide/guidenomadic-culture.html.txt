<h2>Nomadic Life in Mongolia</h2>

<p>
Mongolia is one of the last countries where traditional nomadic life is still alive.
For thousands of years Mongolian families have moved across the vast steppes with their livestock.
</p>

<img src="../images/ger-life.jpg">

<p>
Nomads live in a traditional dwelling called a <b>Ger</b>.
It is a circular tent made from wood and felt, designed to be assembled and moved easily.
The ger is warm in winter and cool in summer.
</p>

<h2>Livestock & Daily Life</h2>

<p>
Mongolian nomads herd five main animals known as the "Five Snouts":
horses, sheep, goats, camels and cattle.
Their lives revolve around caring for animals and moving according to seasons.
</p>

<img src="../images/mongolia-horses.jpg">

<h2>Hospitality</h2>

<p>
Hospitality is one of the most important traditions in Mongolia.
Travelers visiting a nomadic family are often welcomed with milk tea,
dairy products and traditional meals.
</p>
